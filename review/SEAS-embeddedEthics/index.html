<embedded>EthicCS @ Harvard: bringing ethical reasoning into the computer science curriculum, because ethics and social responsibility are part of the computer scientist’s work.

Ethical reasoning

Ethical reasoning is an essential skill for today’s computer scientists. Embedded EthiCS teaches students how to think through the ethical and social implications of their work. Our distributive pedagogy model embeds philosophers directly into courses across the standard computer science curriculum, where they develop and teach a module on an ethical issue raised by the course material.

    Novel computing technologies often improve our lives, but they can also affect them in ways that are harmful or unjust.

  • The Embedded EthiCS Advantage

Meet the Team

Embedded EthiCS is inherently interdisciplinary. Understanding, evaluating, and successfully defending arguments about what should be done falls within the purview of the normative disciplines, most notably ethics, a subfield of philosophy. Harvard Computer Science and Philosophy Department faculty have been partnering to develop the Embedded EthiCS curriculum.

Repository of Open Source Course Modules

The Embedded EthiCS approach adds short ethics modules to computer science courses in the core computer science curriculum. By embedding ethics broadly across the curriculum, this approach meets three goals for computer science students:

Ethical sciences

The extent to which ethical issues permeate almost all areas of computer science.

Ethical industry

The variety of concrete ethical issues and problems that arise across the field.

Ethical experience

The repeated experiences of reasoning through those issues and communicating their positions effectively.

Access our repository of open source course modules

Embedded EthiCS: Integrating Ethics Broadly Across Computer Science Education

"Computer science education can help meet these challenges by making ethical reasoning about computing technologies a central element in the curriculum. Students can learn to think not only about what technology they could create, but also whether they should create that technology. Learning to reason this way requires courses unlike those currently standard in computer science curricula."

Teaching Lab

Our mission is to work with faculty to support the SEAS academic plan and to bolster educational excellence throughout all undergraduate engineering activities.
The SEAS academic plan emphasizes the need for hands-on engineering experiences and student design projects. The Active Learning Laboratories are expected to take the lead and excel in supporting these student experiences. Our support team is composed of full-time engineer/scientist staff members across many disciplines
