Embedded EthiCS @ Harvard
Bringing ethical reasoning into the computer science curriculum, because ethics and social responsibility are part of the computer scientist’s work.

Ethical reasoning is an essential skill for today’s computer scientists. Embedded EthiCS teaches students how to think through the ethical and social implications of their work. Our distributed pedagogy embeds philosophers directly into courses across the standard computer science curriculum to teach a module on an ethical issue raised by the course material.

  • The Embedded EthiCS Mission

    Novel computing technologies often improve our lives, but they can also affect them in ways that are harmful or unjust.

    Learn about the Embedded EthiCS Mission

  • The Embedded EthiCS Strategy

    Embedded EthiCS meets the challenge of making ethical reasoning integral to computer science education with a distributed pedagogy that embeds philosophers directly into standard computer science courses across the curriculum.

    Learn about the Embedded EthiCS Strategy

  • The Embedded EthiCS Advantage

    Our distributed pedagogy approach to teaching ethics in computer science habituates students to think of ethical and social concerns as central to their work as computer scientists.

    Learn about the Embedded EthiCS Advantage

  • The Embedded EthiCS Skill Set

    Rather than advocate a one-size-fits-all approach to ethical problems, the Embedded EthiCS teaches students to consider not merely what technologies they could create, but what technologies they should create.

    Learn about the Embedded EthiCS Skill Set
